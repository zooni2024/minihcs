### Question 5: "What role does corn play in the livestock industry, and why is its byproduct considered valuable for feeding cows?"
**Suggested Answer:**
> Corn is a cornerstone of the livestock industry, primarily because of its high starch content, which provides a concentrated source of energy for growth and milk production. However, the value of corn extends far beyond the whole grain. 
>
> **The Role of Corn Byproducts:**
> When corn is processed for ethanol (Distillers Dried Grains - DDGS) or corn syrup (Corn Gluten Feed), the starch is removed, but the protein, fat, and fiber remainâ€”often in much higher concentrations.
>
> **Why Byproducts Are Highly Valued for Cows:**
> 1. **Protein Powerhouse:** DDGS can contain up to 30% protein, making it a superior supplement to standard forage.
> 2. **Rumen-Friendly:** Because the starch has been removed during processing, these byproducts are less likely to cause digestive issues like acidosis compared to whole corn.
> 3. **Cost-Effective:** Byproducts are often more affordable than whole grains while providing similar or better nutritional profiles.
> 4. **Digestible Fiber:** Cows are ruminants designed to process fiber, and corn byproducts provide a form of "highly digestible fiber" that aids in rumen health.
>
